Frequently Asked Questions

Yes, NayePankh Foundation is an officially registered Section 8 non-profit organization incorporated under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013, in India. We operate with strict compliance, transparency, and governance across all our educational, medical, and relief initiatives.

Our core mission is to empower underprivileged Indian communities by addressing foundational struggles at the grassroots level. We actively work across food security, primary education, animal rescue, hygiene camps, and vocational skill-development workshops to create a highly supportive and self-sufficient future.

We actively conduct field volunteering drives, supply runs, and community workshops in Udaipur, Ajmer, Jaipur, and surrounding rural communities in Rajasthan. As a growing youth-led foundation, we aim to scale our projects to other tier-2 and tier-3 locations across India in the near future.

Becoming a volunteer is simple! You can click the "Join Drive" button on our menu navigation or visit our dedicated volunteer page to fill out the onboarding application. Our volunteer coordination team reviews submissions weekly and will reach out to schedule your welcome orientation.

No prior experience is necessary. We welcome volunteers from all backgrounds who share a genuine drive for social good. New volunteers are paired with experienced lead coordinators and provided with complete on-field guidelines during their initial campaigns.

Absolutely! Students represent the vibrant core of our volunteering ecosystem. We customize flexible weekend drive schedules and academic break projects so students can contribute meaningfully to social change without affecting their academic obligations.

Yes, we offer multiple remote digital volunteering roles. You can support our cause from anywhere in the world by contributing in graphic design, website development, content writing, social media management, or digital awareness campaigns.

For field campaigns, we recommend a flexible commitment of 3 to 4 hours per week, typically on weekend mornings. For remote digital roles, hours are highly project-based and can be managed completely at your own pace during the week.

Yes, NayePankh Foundation provides an official Certificate of Appreciation to all active volunteers. To qualify, volunteers must complete their designated campaign terms, active field hours, or digital project deliverables successfully.

Donations can be processed safely through our secure portal using UPI, major debit/credit cards, net banking, or digital wallets. For monthly recurring sponsorships or direct wire bank transfers, please reach out to our finance desk directly.

Security is our utmost priority. All transactions are routed through industry-leading, certified payment gateways using advanced 256-bit SSL encryption. We do not store any sensitive card information or bank credentials on our servers.

Every rupee contributed goes directly toward procuring food ingredients, educational books, learning tablets, rescue jackets, and medical kits. We maintain a zero-waste policy and publish audit logs, cost-breakdowns, and supply-run distributions regularly.

Yes, we actively welcome in-kind donations! We accept fresh or dry grains, packed food items, clean winter clothing, school notebooks, and writing materials. Please submit a pickup request on our contact page to coordinate collection.

Yes, donations made by Indian taxpayers to NayePankh Foundation are eligible for tax deductions under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act. A formal 80G tax receipt is emailed to you along with your annual contribution statement.

Through our "Nourishing Communities" initiative, volunteers cook and package fresh, nutritious meal boxes. These are distributed weekly in identified slum areas, focusing on delivering stable nutrition to vulnerable young children and senior citizens.

Our "Wings of Knowledge" initiative organizes open-air community classrooms for children who lack school access. We teach elementary subjects, run basic computer workshops, and provide scholarship materials to prepare students for formal schooling.

Our "Dignity & Health" camps educate adolescent girls and women in rural villages on reproductive health. We dismantle structural social taboos, provide private clinical counseling, and distribute free packs of high-quality bio-degradable sanitary napkins.

Corporations can align with us through CSR grants, sponsoring specific community initiatives, or organizing corporate volunteer days. Please email our CSR partnerships desk at csr@nayepankh.org to request our project proposal brochure.

Yes, we actively collaborate with academic institutions to host campus fundraisers, food collection drives, and student internships. Student group leaders can submit a collaboration query on our contact page to set up a project.

You can set up a personal online fundraiser on social media or request marketing materials from us to organize a school, community, or workplace donation campaign. Contact our campaign support team to receive fundraising setup guides.
